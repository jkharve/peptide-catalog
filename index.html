Sleep Support

Foundational sleep optimization tools for recovery, restoration, and whole-body wellness

Sleep Dealer
Mouth Tape

Topical

Sleep Dealer mouth tape is a precision-designed adhesive strip worn over the lips during sleep to encourage nasal breathing — the physiologically optimal breathing pattern for rest and recovery. Mouth breathing during sleep bypasses the nasal cavity's natural filtration and humidification system, leading to dry mouth, disrupted sleep architecture, elevated cortisol, and impaired oxygen exchange. Nasal breathing, by contrast, produces nitric oxide in the sinuses — a vasodilator that improves blood oxygenation, lowers resting heart rate, and supports deeper, more restorative sleep cycles.

Sleep Dealer's formulation features a hydrating lip mask layer built into the adhesive — so you wake with softer lips, not just better sleep. A simple intervention with compounding nightly returns.

Subcutaneous Injection Guide

Where to Pin

Optimal injection sites for subcutaneous peptide administration, with guidance on technique and rotation protocol.


01
Periumbilical Abdomen

Within approximately 2 inches surrounding the navel — avoiding the belly button itself. The most abundant subcutaneous fat depot on most bodies, offering consistent tissue depth and excellent compound absorption. Preferred primary site for most peptide protocols.


02
Lateral Abdomen (Love Handles)

The flanks along the lateral abdomen provide ample subcutaneous tissue with minimal sensitivity. An excellent rotation site that gives the primary abdominal zone adequate recovery time between injections.


03
Upper Glute

The upper outer quadrant of the gluteal region offers a substantial subcutaneous fat layer with minimal nerve density. Well-tolerated for larger volume injections and a commonly preferred site for those with lower abdominal sensitivity.


04
Posterior Upper Arm (Tricep)

The back of the upper arm, in the tricep region, has reliable subcutaneous tissue that absorbs well. Slightly less accessible for self-injection but manageable with practice. Useful for daily rotation variety.


05
Anterior Thigh

The outer top portion of the thigh — a large, accessible surface with consistent subcutaneous depth. Easily self-administered and comfortable for most users. A reliable rotation site, especially when other zones need rest.

⟳  Always Rotate Injection Sites

Repeated injections to the same location cause lipohypertrophy — a hardening of subcutaneous tissue that impairs absorption and causes visible scarring. Systematically rotate through all available sites, spacing consecutive injections by at least 1–2 cm. Maintain a simple log to track rotation if needed. Consistent rotation ensures optimal absorption, prevents tissue damage, and preserves site integrity long-term.

Needle Angle

Use a 45° insertion angle for subcutaneous delivery with a short needle (4–6mm). Pinch the skin slightly to create a skin tent if tissue is thin.

Temperature

Allow refrigerated compounds to reach room temperature for 5–10 minutes before injection. Cold solutions can cause localized stinging and slower absorption.

Injection Depth

Target the subcutaneous fat layer — beneath the skin but above muscle. Correct depth produces minimal resistance; muscle injection stings more sharply.

Post-Injection

Apply gentle pressure with a dry cotton ball — do not rub. Rubbing disperses the compound too rapidly and increases bruising risk at the site.
